title: "Collect payments with SEPA direct debit"
description: "Set up SEPA Direct Debit in TRONVoice: request mandates from clients, create collection batches, and collect invoice amounts automatically via Mollie."
---

SEPA Direct Debit (automatische incasso) lets you collect invoice payments directly from a client's bank account — without the client having to take any action each time. The client authorizes you once via a mandate, and you can then collect invoice amounts in batches.

<Note>
  SEPA Direct Debit requires your Mollie account to be connected and SEPA configured for your administration. Contact your account administrator if this feature is not available.
</Note>

## Overview of the process

1. **Request a mandate** — send the client an authorization link by email
2. **Client accepts** — the client completes a small verification payment to confirm their IBAN
3. **Create a batch** — select eligible invoices and schedule a collection date
4. **Send pre-notification** — TRONVoice notifies clients at least 14 days before collection
5. **Process the batch** — TRONVoice submits the batch to Mollie for collection

---

## Step 1: Request a mandate from a client

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the client record">
    Go to **Clients** and open the client you want to set up for automatic direct debit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a mandate request">
    Click **Send mandate request**. TRONVoice creates a mandate record and sends the client an email with a link to authorize direct debit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Client authorizes the mandate">
    The client opens the link and sees your company name, logo, and a short explanation. They click through to Mollie, complete a small one-time verification payment to confirm their bank account, and the mandate is registered.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The mandate link is valid for **48 hours**. If the client does not complete it in time, you can resend the request from the client record.

<Warning>
  Only one pending mandate request per client is allowed at a time. If there is already an open request, cancel it before sending a new one.
</Warning>

### Mandate statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Mandate request sent; client has not yet completed authorization |
| **Active** | Mandate is valid; client can be included in direct debit batches |
| **Revoked** | Mandate was revoked; client cannot be collected until a new mandate is active |

---

## Step 2: Create a mandate manually (optional)

If you have a paper mandate signed by the client, you can register it manually instead of sending an email request.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the client record">
    Go to **Clients** and open the relevant client.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a manual mandate">
    Click **Create manual mandate** and fill in:
    - Account holder name
    - IBAN
    - BIC (optional)
    - Signature date
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the mandate">
    The mandate is immediately set to **Active** and the client is enabled for automatic direct debit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Step 3: Create a direct debit batch

Once clients have active mandates, you can collect invoices by creating a batch.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Direct debit batches">
    Navigate to **Payments → Direct debit batches**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new batch">
    Click **New batch** and select a collection date. The date must be in the future to allow time for the mandatory pre-notification.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review eligible invoices">
    TRONVoice shows all outstanding invoices for clients with an active mandate. Add the invoices you want to include in this batch.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove items if needed">
    You can remove individual items from the batch while it is still **Open**. Once a batch is locked, items cannot be removed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Batches are numbered automatically in the format `BATCH-{year}-{sequence}` — for example, `BATCH-2025-001`.

---

## Pre-notification requirement

SEPA regulations require that clients be notified at least **14 days** before a direct debit collection. TRONVoice handles this automatically — a pre-notification email is sent to each client included in the batch when the batch is submitted.

<Warning>
  You are responsible for ensuring the 14-day legal notice period is met before submitting the batch to Mollie. Do not set a collection date less than 14 days in the future.
</Warning>

---

## Step 4: Submit and process the batch

Once you are satisfied with the batch contents, submit it for processing. TRONVoice sends the payments to Mollie, which initiates the direct debit collections on the scheduled date.

After collection, TRONVoice updates each invoice based on the result:
- **Successful** — invoice is marked as paid
- **Failed or charged back** — invoice remains open; you can follow up with the client

---

## Managing mandates

### Resend a mandate request

If the client did not complete the authorization in time, go to the client record and click **Resend request**. This resends the email with a new authorization link.

### Revoke a mandate

To stop automatic direct debit for a client, open the client record and click **Revoke mandate**. The mandate status changes to **Revoked** and the client is immediately excluded from future batches.

<Tip>
  Under SEPA rules, clients have the right to dispute a direct debit collection within 8 weeks of the transaction date. Inform clients of this right when you request their mandate.
</Tip>
