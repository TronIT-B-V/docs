---
title: "Connect your bank account to TRONVoice"
description: "Link your bank account via Ponto to automatically import and match transactions against open invoices in TRONVoice, or import MT940 files."
---

Connecting a bank account lets TRONVoice import your transactions automatically and match incoming payments to open invoices. Instead of manually marking invoices as paid, matched transactions update invoice statuses for you.

Bank connections use **Ponto** by Isabel Group, a certified PSD2 provider. TRONVoice receives read-only access to your transactions — it cannot initiate payments or modify your account.

<Note>
  Each bank connection costs **€3.99 per month**. Connections are billed separately from your regular TRONVoice credits and can be cancelled at any time. You need an active bank connection subscription before you can connect your first account.
</Note>

---

## Supported banks

Ponto supports major Dutch and Belgian banks, including ING, Rabobank, ABN AMRO, SNS, Triodos, KBC, BNP Paribas Fortis, and others. When you start the connection flow, you can select your bank from the full list of available institutions.

---

## Connect a bank account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Bank connections">
    Navigate to **Payments → Bank connections**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate a bank connection subscription">
    If you have no active subscription yet, click **Activate Smart Banking** and complete the subscription checkout. Each slot you purchase covers one bank account connection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add bank">
    Click the **Add bank** button. A modal opens where you can optionally select:
    - An administration to link transactions to
    - Your bank from the list of available institutions (or leave blank to choose during the Ponto authorization flow)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize via Ponto">
    Click **Continue to bank**. You are redirected to Ponto's secure authorization portal, where you log in to your bank and grant read access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connection is created">
    After authorization, you are returned to TRONVoice. The connection is created and an initial sync imports your recent transactions automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Transaction sync

Once connected, TRONVoice syncs your transactions automatically every 6 hours. You can also trigger a manual sync at any time from the bank connection detail page.

The Ponto connection consent is valid for **12 months**. TRONVoice shows a warning when your consent is about to expire (within 7 days). When it expires, you need to reconnect to resume syncing.

<Note>
  Auto-sync and auto-match settings can only be changed by administrators.
</Note>

### Connection statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Active** | Connected and syncing normally |
| **Pending** | Connection being set up |
| **Inactive / Error** | Connection lost; reconnection required |
| **Expired** | Ponto consent expired; re-authorize to resume |

If a connection shows as **Inactive** or **Error**, click **Reconnect** from the connection detail page to restart the authorization flow.

## Match transactions to invoices

After transactions are imported, TRONVoice displays them in **Payments → Bank transactions**. Unmatched transactions appear in the default view; matched and ignored ones appear under **Processed**.

### Automatic matching

When auto-match is enabled, TRONVoice attempts to match each imported transaction to an open invoice using:

1. Invoice number in the transaction description or payment reference (highest priority)
2. Client IBAN matching the counterparty account
3. Client name similarity
4. Amount matching

When a transaction is automatically matched, the linked invoice is marked as paid.

### Manual matching

For transactions that are not automatically matched, you can match them manually:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the transaction">
    Click the unmatched transaction in **Bank transactions**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the invoice">
    TRONVoice shows a ranked list of potential matching invoices based on description, IBAN, client name, and amount. Select the correct invoice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the match">
    Optionally adjust the matched amount (for partial payments), then confirm. The invoice is updated accordingly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

A single transaction can be matched to multiple invoices — useful when one bank transfer covers several invoices. A single invoice can also be partially matched across multiple transactions.

### Ignore transactions

If a transaction is not related to any invoice (for example, a bank fee or an internal transfer), you can ignore it. Ignored transactions move to the **Processed** view and no longer appear in the unmatched list. You can un-ignore a transaction at any time to match it later.

## Import bank statements manually (MT940)

If you prefer not to use a live bank connection, or if your bank is not supported by Ponto, you can import transactions manually using MT940 bank statement files.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Export an MT940 file from your bank">
    Log in to your bank's online banking portal and export a statement in MT940 format. Most Dutch banks support this format.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the import page">
    In TRONVoice, go to **Payments → Bank transactions** and click **Import**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your administration and upload the file">
    Choose the administration the transactions belong to, select your MT940 file (maximum 10 MB), and click **Import**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the results">
    TRONVoice shows how many transactions were imported and how many were automatically matched. Unmatched transactions appear in the regular transaction list for manual matching.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  MT940 import runs auto-matching immediately after import. You can review and adjust any automatic matches from the transaction list.
</Tip>

## Remove a bank connection

To remove a connection, open it from **Bank connections** and click **Delete**. This revokes TRONVoice's access at Ponto and permanently deletes all imported transactions for that connection. Invoices that were marked as paid via matched transactions are not affected.

<Warning>
  Deleting a connection also deletes all associated transactions. This action cannot be undone.
</Warning>
