An **incassobatch** (direct debit batch) groups outstanding invoices into a single SEPA collection run. When you send an invoice to a client who has a valid SEPA mandate, TRONVoice automatically adds it to an open batch. You review the batch, set a collection date, and submit it — Mollie then collects each amount on the scheduled day.

<Note>
  Incasso batches require SEPA Direct Debit to be configured for your administration. If the **Direct debit batches** menu is not visible, SEPA is not yet set up. See [Collect payments with SEPA direct debit](/payments/sepa-direct-debit) for how mandates and SEPA settings work.
</Note>

## How batches relate to SEPA mandates

A batch can only collect from clients who have authorized you in advance. The relationship is:

1. A client authorizes a **SEPA mandate** — once, via email or a manual paper mandate.
2. When you send an invoice to that client, TRONVoice checks the mandate and the client's **auto direct debit** setting.
3. If the client is eligible, the invoice is added to an **open** batch as a batch item.
4. You submit the batch, and Mollie collects each item using the client's mandate.

Invoices for clients without a valid mandate are never added to a batch — you collect those the usual way, for example with a [Mollie payment link](/payments/mollie).

---

## How invoices enter a batch

Most batch items are created automatically. When you send an invoice, TRONVoice adds it to a batch if **all** of the following are true:

- The client has **auto direct debit** enabled and a valid mandate
- The invoice is in euros, has a positive amount, and is not a credit invoice
- SEPA is configured for the administration

TRONVoice calculates a collection date (invoice date plus the pre-notification period, snapped to your preferred collection day) and finds an **open** batch for that date — or creates a new one. Batches are numbered automatically as `BATCH-{year}-{sequence}`, for example `BATCH-2025-001`.

You can also add invoices to a batch manually — see [Add an invoice](#add-an-invoice-manually) below.

## Create a batch manually

You usually don't need to create batches yourself, but you can prepare one for a specific collection date.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Direct debit batches">
    Navigate to **Payments → Direct debit batches**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the new batch dialog">
    Click **New batch** (**Nieuwe batch**).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a collection date">
    Pick an **Incassodatum** (collection date). It must be at least **3 days** in the future to allow time for the pre-notification.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    TRONVoice creates an empty open batch — or reuses an existing open batch for that date — and opens it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Review a batch

Open a batch from the list to see its details:

- **Batchnummer** (batch number) and **Status**
- **Incassodatum** (collection date)
- **Totaalbedrag** (total amount) and number of items
- A table of items showing **Factuurnummer** (invoice number), **Klant** (client), **Bedrag** (amount), and item status

### Batch statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Open** | You can still add, remove, and move items, and change the collection date |
| **Locked** | Batch submitted; pre-notifications sent; contents frozen |
| **Processing** | Submitted to Mollie; collections in progress |
| **Completed** | All collections finished |
| **Partially failed** | Some collections succeeded, others failed or were charged back |

### Item statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Queued; not yet sent to Mollie |
| **Submitted** | Sent to Mollie; awaiting result |
| **Paid** | Collected successfully; the invoice is marked as paid |
| **Failed** | Collection failed; the invoice stays open |
| **Charged back** | Client reversed the collection; the invoice stays open |

## Adjust an open batch

While a batch is **Open**, you can change its contents freely. Once it is locked, none of these actions are available.

### Add an invoice manually

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Add invoice">
    On the batch detail page, click **Factuur toevoegen** (add invoice).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search for an invoice">
    Search by invoice number or client name. Only sent invoices for clients with auto direct debit enabled — and not already in a batch — appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add it">
    Select the invoice. TRONVoice adds it to this batch and updates the totals. If the client has no valid mandate, the invoice cannot be added.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Remove an item

Click **Verwijderen** (remove) next to a batch item. The invoice is detached from the batch and returns to its normal outstanding state. If the batch becomes empty, TRONVoice deletes it automatically.

### Move an item to another batch

Click **Verplaatsen** (move) next to an item. You can move it to another open batch or create a new batch with its own collection date. Both batches must be open.

### Change the collection date

Click **Incassodatum wijzigen** (change collection date). The new date must be at least 3 days in the future. This reschedules the whole batch.

<Warning>
  SEPA rules require clients to be notified before a collection. Keep the collection date far enough ahead to meet the pre-notification period configured for your administration (typically 14 days). The 3-day minimum is a technical floor, not the legal notice period.
</Warning>

## Submit a batch

When the batch contents are correct, submit it for collection.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Submit batch">
    On the batch detail page, click **Batch indienen** (submit batch). A batch must contain at least one item.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Batch is locked">
    The status changes to **Locked** and the contents are frozen. You can no longer add, remove, or move items.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pre-notifications are sent">
    TRONVoice emails each client in the batch a SEPA pre-notification stating the amount, collection date, masked IBAN, and mandate reference.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mollie collects on the scheduled date">
    The batch moves to **Processing** and TRONVoice submits each item to Mollie, which collects on the collection date.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After collection, each item is updated with its result. A **Paid** item marks its invoice as paid. **Failed** and **charged back** items leave the invoice open so you can follow up.

### Sync results from Mollie

For a batch that is **Processing**, **Completed**, or **Partially failed**, click **Synchroniseren** (sync) to pull the latest payment results from Mollie. This also surfaces chargebacks on items that were previously paid.

<Note>
  Syncing is rate-limited to once per minute per batch. TRONVoice also updates results automatically via Mollie webhooks, so a manual sync is mainly useful when you want an immediate refresh.
</Note>

## The incasso dashboard widget

The dashboard shows an **incasso** widget that summarizes the state of your direct debit pipeline at a glance:

- **Next batch** — the upcoming open or locked batch, with its collection date and item count
- **Pending mandates** — the number of clients with a mandate request that has not yet been authorized
- **Recent failures** — the most recent failed or charged-back collections, with the invoice number and client

Use the widget to spot mandates that need a nudge and collections that need follow-up. If SEPA is not configured for the administration, the widget shows a setup prompt instead.

<Tip>
  Charged-back collections need attention: the client reversed the payment, so the invoice is open again. Under SEPA rules, clients can dispute a direct debit for up to 8 weeks. Contact the client to arrange payment another way.
</Tip>
