---
title: "Inventory and stock management"
description: "Track stock levels per product, organise stock across locations, adjust quantities, transfer stock between locations, and review a complete mutation history."
---

For products you sell as physical goods, TRONVoice can track stock. Once stock tracking is enabled on a product, every change in its quantity is recorded as a **voorraadmutatie** (stock mutation) — so you always have a complete, auditable history of how the level reached its current value.

## Enabling stock tracking

Stock tracking is set per product. On the product form, turn on **Track inventory** and set:

- **Current stock** — the starting quantity.
- **Minimum stock** — a threshold that triggers a low-stock warning.
- **Stock location** — the location where this product is stored.
- **Behaviour at zero stock** — choose *warn* (allow but show a warning) or *block* (prevent adding the product to an invoice once stock reaches zero).

Stock tracking can be turned on or off at any time. Disabling it stops future tracking but keeps the existing mutation history.

## The stock overview

Go to **Voorraad** (Inventory) to open the stock overview. It lists every product with stock tracking enabled, showing the current level, location, and stock status.

Use the controls to narrow the list:

- **Search** by product name or article number.
- **Filter by location** to see what is stored where.
- **Filter by stock status** to surface products that are low or out of stock.

From the overview you can adjust a product's stock or start a transfer without leaving the page.

## Stock locations

Stock locations (**Voorraadlocaties**) represent the physical places where you keep stock — a warehouse, a shop, or a van. They are shared across your whole organisation.

Manage them under **Voorraadlocaties** (Stock locations):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a location">
    Click **New location**, enter a **name** (required) and an optional **description**, and save. Location names must be unique within your organisation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign products">
    Set a product's **stock location** on the product form. Each stock-tracked product belongs to one location.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deactivate when no longer used">
    Deactivating a location hides it from selectors but keeps existing data intact.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  A stock location can only be deleted when no products are assigned to it. If products are still linked, TRONVoice blocks the deletion and tells you how many.
</Warning>

## How stock changes

Every change to a product's stock is recorded as a **voorraadmutatie**. Each mutation stores the quantity, the stock level before and after, the type of change, an optional reason, and — where relevant — the user or invoice involved. Mutation types are:

| Type | When it happens | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Factuur | A product is invoiced | Stock decreases |
| Creditnota | A credit note is issued | Stock increases |
| Factuurwijziging | An invoice line quantity changes | Stock adjusts to the difference |
| Beginvoorraad | Opening stock is set when tracking starts | Sets the initial level |
| Handmatige aanpassing | You adjust stock manually | Increases or decreases |
| Correctie | A correction entry | Increases or decreases |
| Transfer (uit) | Stock leaves a location in a transfer | Stock decreases |
| Transfer (in) | Stock arrives at a location in a transfer | Stock increases |

## Adjusting stock manually

When you receive a delivery, write off damaged goods, or correct a count, adjust stock directly from the product or the stock overview.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the adjustment dialog">
    On a stock-tracked product, choose to adjust the stock.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose add or remove">
    Select **Toevoegen** (Add) or **Verwijderen** (Remove).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the quantity and reason">
    Enter the quantity and a reason (minimum 3 characters). You can pick a preset reason or type your own:

    | Adding | Removing |
    |---|---|
    | Purchase / delivery received | Sale / issue |
    | Return from client | Damaged / unusable |
    | Correction after count | Expired |
    | | Correction after count |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    The dialog previews the resulting stock level. Confirm to record the mutation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  A reason is always required for a manual adjustment. It is stored on the mutation so the history stays meaningful.
</Note>

## Transferring stock between locations

To move stock from one location to another, use a stock transfer. A transfer records two linked mutations: a *Transfer (uit)* at the source and a *Transfer (in)* at the destination.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the transfer dialog">
    Start a transfer from the stock overview or from a product.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose source, destination, and quantity">
    Select the **source product**, the **destination location**, and the **quantity** to move. Add an optional note. The dialog previews the source product's stock after the transfer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the transfer">
    Confirm. Stock decreases at the source and increases at the destination.
  </Step>
</Steps>

At the destination, TRONVoice finds a matching product (by article number, then by name) and adds the quantity to it. If no match exists, a new product is created at the destination location with the same details, so the destination always has something to receive the stock.

<Tip>
  Only products with stock tracking, a location, and a positive stock level can be used as a transfer source.
</Tip>

## Viewing a product's mutation history

Every mutation is kept permanently. To review the full history of a product, open the product and view its **stock mutations**. The history is paginated and shows, for each entry, the date, the type, the quantity, the level before and after, the reason, and the linked invoice or user where applicable.

This gives you a complete audit trail — from opening stock through every sale, return, adjustment, and transfer.
