Products are the line items you add to invoices, quotes, and subscriptions. Defining them once means you can select them by name instead of typing descriptions and prices from scratch each time.

## Creating a product

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the products list">
    Go to **Products** in the main navigation and click **New product**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the product details">
    The fields available on the product form:

    | Field | Notes |
    |---|---|
    | Name | Required. Shown on invoices. |
    | Article number | Optional internal or supplier reference code. |
    | Description | Default line description used when adding to an invoice. |
    | Price (excl. VAT) | Standard selling price per unit. |
    | Cost price | Your purchase cost. Used to calculate margin (not shown on invoices). |
    | Unit | The unit of measure: hour, day, piece, time, m², m³, km, or kg. |
    | VAT rate | Applied to this product by default: 21%, 9%, or 0%. |
    | Category | Optional product category for filtering and grouping. |
    | Stock tracking | Enable to track inventory levels for this product. |
    | Active | Inactive products are hidden from invoice line selectors. |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the product">
    Click **Save**. The product is immediately available when adding lines to invoices, quotes, and subscriptions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Product categories

Categories help you organise a large product list and filter quickly when creating invoices.

To manage categories, go to **Products → Categories**. Each category has a name, an optional description, and an active/inactive toggle. Deactivating a category hides it from the category selector but does not affect existing products assigned to it.

<Tip>
  Use categories to group related products — for example, "Consultancy", "Software licences", or "Travel costs". You can filter the products list by category to find the right item faster.
</Tip>

## Price and VAT settings

Each product has its own VAT rate. When you add a product to an invoice, the rate from the product record is used as the default — you can still change it on the invoice line if needed.

The client's VAT settings (reversed VAT or VAT-exempt) always take precedence over the product's default rate. If a client is set to reversed VAT, all lines on their invoices will use reversed VAT regardless of the product setting.

## Inventory tracking

You can enable stock management on a per-product basis. When enabled, TRONVoice tracks the current stock level and records a mutation every time the level changes.

### Enabling stock tracking

On the product form, turn on **Track inventory**. You can then set:

- **Current stock** — the starting quantity.
- **Minimum stock** — a threshold that triggers a low-stock warning.
- **Stock location** — the physical location where this product is stored.
- **Behaviour at zero stock** — choose between *warn* (allow but show a warning) or *block* (prevent adding to an invoice when stock reaches zero).

### How stock changes

Stock levels change automatically in these situations:

| Event | Effect on stock |
|---|---|
| Invoice sent | Stock decreases by the quantity on the invoice line |
| Credit note | Stock increases by the credited quantity |
| Invoice line changed | Stock is adjusted to reflect the difference |
| Manual adjustment | You enter a quantity directly on the product record |
| Opening stock entry | Set the initial stock level when you first enable tracking |
| Transfer | Move stock between locations |

### Viewing the mutations log

Every stock change is recorded as a mutation with a timestamp, quantity before and after, the type of change, and the user who made it. Open a product and go to the **Stock** tab to see the full history.

<Note>
  Stock tracking is optional and can be enabled or disabled at any time. Disabling it stops future tracking but keeps the existing mutation history.
</Note>

## Bulk actions

From the products list you can select multiple products and apply bulk actions:

- **Activate** — make selected products available on invoices again.
- **Deactivate** — hide selected products from invoice line selectors without deleting them.
- **Delete** — permanently remove selected products. Products that have been used on invoices cannot be deleted.

## Importing products from Excel or CSV

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the import page">
    From the products list, click **Import** and download the template file to see the required column layout.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare your file">
    Fill in your products using the template. The **name** column is required. Include optional columns for price, VAT rate, article number, description, and category.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload and confirm">
    Upload your file. A preview shows how TRONVoice will create each product. Confirm to complete the import.
  </Step>
</Steps>
