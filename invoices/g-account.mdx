---
title: "G-account (G-rekening) invoicing"
description: "Split invoice payments into a free amount and a G-account amount. Set up G-rekening for an administration, override it per client, and control the split per invoice line."
---

A **G-rekening** (G-account) is a blocked bank account used in the Dutch construction and staffing sectors. When you hire out staff or work as a subcontractor, part of the invoice is paid into your G-account instead of your normal account. That blocked balance is reserved to guarantee payroll-tax (loonheffing) and VAT obligations, which protects the client from being held liable for them.

TRONVoice supports this by splitting the payment instructions on the invoice into two parts:

- A **free amount** (vrij bedrag) paid to your normal IBAN.
- A **G-account amount** (G-rekening bedrag) paid to your G-account IBAN.

<Info>
  G-account invoicing is only relevant if you hire out personnel or subcontract labour — typically construction firms, staffing agencies, and payrolling companies. If this does not apply to your business, leave G-rekening disabled.
</Info>

## Enabling G-rekening for an administration

G-rekening is configured per administration in the administration settings.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the administration settings">
    Go to **Settings → Administration** and find the **G-rekening** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Turn on G-rekening">
    Enable **G-rekening actief** (G-account active). This makes TRONVoice show split payment instructions on this administration's invoices.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the G-account details">
    | Field | Notes |
    |---|---|
    | **G-rekening IBAN** (G-account IBAN) | The IBAN of your blocked G-account. Shown on the invoice so the client knows where to send the G-account part. |
    | **Storting percentage** (Deposit percentage) | The default share of the invoice amount paid into the G-account, as a whole-number percentage (for example 25). |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Save the administration. New invoices for this administration will now calculate and show the split.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you enable G-rekening but leave the G-account IBAN empty, invoices still show the split amounts, but the payment instruction states that the G-account IBAN is available on request instead of printing it.
</Note>

## Overriding the percentage and IBAN per client

Some clients agree a different G-account percentage, or require a different G-account IBAN. You can override both on the client record.

On the client edit form, the **G-rekening** section appears only when the administration has G-rekening enabled. It shows the administration's default percentage and IBAN for reference, and lets you set:

- **Percentage (overschrijving)** — a client-specific G-account percentage.
- **G-rekening IBAN (overschrijving)** — a client-specific G-account IBAN.

<Tip>
  Leave an override field empty to keep using the administration default. The client form shows the inherited value as a placeholder so you always know what applies.
</Tip>

The effective values are resolved in this order:

1. The value set on the **client**, if any.
2. Otherwise, the value set on the **administration**.

## Choosing which lines count towards the G-account

The G-account split is calculated per invoice line. Each line has a **G-rek.** (G-account) checkbox, shown only when the administration has G-rekening enabled.

- Only lines with the G-account checkbox ticked are included in the G-account amount.
- Lines with the checkbox cleared are paid entirely to the free amount.
- New lines have the checkbox ticked by default.

This lets you keep, for example, material costs out of the G-account split while including labour.

<Note>
  Note rows (text-only lines without pricing) are never included in the G-account calculation.
</Note>

## How the split is calculated

For an invoice with G-rekening enabled and a percentage above zero, TRONVoice:

1. Adds up the subtotal and VAT of every line that has the G-account checkbox ticked.
2. Applies any invoice-level discount to that subtotal.
3. Multiplies the discounted subtotal by the G-account percentage to get the **G-account amount**.
4. Subtracts the G-account amount from the invoice total to get the **free amount**.

The free amount and G-account amount always add up to the full invoice total, so the client pays the same total — just split across two accounts.

## How the split appears on the invoice

When G-rekening applies, the invoice PDF replaces the standard payment line with split payment instructions. They tell the client to pay, before the due date:

- the **free amount** to your normal IBAN, and
- the **G-account amount** to your G-account IBAN (or, if no G-account IBAN is configured, a note that the IBAN is available on request),

both quoting the invoice number.

<Warning>
  The G-account details are captured on the invoice when it is created, so the split shown on a sent invoice reflects the settings that applied at that time. Changing the administration or client G-rekening settings later does not retroactively alter already-sent invoices.
</Warning>
