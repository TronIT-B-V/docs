---
title: "Schedule an invoice for later"
description: "Set a TRONVoice invoice to send automatically at a future date and time, edit a planned send, or cancel it before it goes out."
---

TRONVoice can send an invoice automatically at a future date and time. Scheduling is useful for month-end billing cycles, or when you want to prepare invoices in advance and have them go out on a fixed day.

## How scheduling works

When you schedule a draft invoice, TRONVoice stores a **planned send date** (**gepland verzend datum**) on the invoice. The invoice stays a **draft** (**concept**) until that moment — its line items can still be edited, and no invoice number is assigned yet.

When the scheduled time arrives, TRONVoice automatically:
- Changes the status to **Verzonden** (Sent) and assigns the permanent invoice number.
- Sends the invoice by email to the recipients you chose.
- Deducts the **€0.65 credit** for sending the invoice.

<Note>
Only **draft** invoices can be scheduled. An invoice that has already been sent cannot be scheduled.
</Note>

## Schedule an invoice

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the draft invoice">
    Go to **Invoices** and open the draft you want to schedule.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the schedule dialog">
    Click **Schedule** (**Inplannen**) — usually shown next to the **Send** button. A date and time picker appears.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the date and time">
    Pick a **date** — today or any day in the future — and a **time**. Times are selected in 15-minute steps (`:00`, `:15`, `:30`, `:45`). The combined date and time must be in the future.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose recipients">
    Select who receives the invoice when it sends:
    - **Send to client** (**Verstuur naar klant**) — sends to the client's email address.
    - **Send to admin** (**Verstuur naar admin**) — sends a copy to your administration's email address.

    Both are enabled by default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Confirm the dialog. The invoice detail page now shows the planned send date and time. The invoice remains a draft until it sends.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
An invoice can only be scheduled if your administration has an **IBAN** configured. Without an IBAN, TRONVoice shows an error and directs you to the administration settings.
</Warning>

## Credit check at scheduling

TRONVoice checks that you have enough credit when you schedule the invoice. If your balance is insufficient, you are redirected to the credit top-up page before the schedule can be saved.

<Note>
The credit is only deducted when the invoice actually sends — not when you schedule it. If your balance runs low before the planned date, top up in time so the scheduled send is not blocked.
</Note>

## Edit a scheduled send

The planned send date is stored on the invoice itself. To change it, open the invoice and schedule it again with the new date, time, or recipients. The new schedule replaces the previous one.

Because a scheduled invoice is still a draft, you can also freely edit its line items, client, dates, and notes right up until it sends.

## Cancel a scheduled send

To stop a scheduled invoice from sending:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the invoice">
    Go to **Invoices** and open the scheduled draft.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cancel the schedule">
    Click **Cancel schedule** (**Annuleer planning**). The planned send date is cleared.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The invoice stays a draft and will no longer send automatically. You can schedule it again later, send it immediately, or leave it as a draft. The cancellation is recorded in the invoice activity log.

<Tip>
Sending an invoice immediately also clears any existing schedule — so if you decide to send earlier than planned, just click **Send** and the scheduled send is dropped automatically.
</Tip>
