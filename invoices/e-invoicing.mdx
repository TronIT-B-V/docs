TRONVoice can produce an invoice in three formats: a standard **PDF**, a **proforma PDF** for drafts, and a structured **UBL e-invoice**. This page explains each format and when to use it.

## What is e-invoicing (e-facturatie)?

An **e-invoice** (e-factuur) is an invoice delivered as structured, machine-readable data rather than as a document a person reads. TRONVoice generates e-invoices in **UBL 2.1** — an XML format that accounting software can import automatically, without manual data entry or scanning.

TRONVoice produces UBL using the **Peppol BIS Billing 3.0** profile, the European standard for cross-border electronic invoicing. The XML includes the sender's and recipient's VAT numbers as Peppol endpoint identifiers (NL VAT scheme).

<Info>
**When does a Dutch business need UBL?** Invoices to the Dutch central government must be delivered electronically — typically as UBL through the Peppol network or a government portal. Many larger organisations and accounting packages also prefer UBL because it imports cleanly. For invoices to ordinary private clients, a PDF is usually enough.
</Info>

## Download a standard PDF

Every invoice can be downloaded as a PDF from the invoice detail page or the invoice list.

- The PDF reflects the invoice exactly as your client sees it, including your logo, VAT breakdown, and payment details.
- Once an invoice is sent, the PDF is stored and reused, so it always matches what was sent.
- If the invoice has the **merge attachments** option enabled, the PDF download combines the invoice and its PDF attachments into one document.

## Download a proforma PDF

A **proforma PDF** is a preview of a draft invoice. Use it when you want to show a client what an invoice will look like before it is officially sent.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a draft invoice">
    Go to **Invoices** and open the draft you want to preview.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the proforma">
    Click **Proforma PDF**. TRONVoice generates a PDF marked as a proforma.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
A proforma PDF is only available for **draft** (**concept**) invoices. Once an invoice is sent, use the standard PDF instead. A proforma is not a valid invoice for VAT or bookkeeping purposes — it carries the temporary `CONCEPT-XXXXX` number.
</Note>

## Export a UBL e-invoice

When UBL export is available for your administration, a **UBL** button appears on the invoice detail page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the invoice">
    Go to **Invoices** and open the invoice you want to export.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click UBL">
    Click **UBL**. TRONVoice generates a UBL 2.1 XML file and downloads it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deliver the file">
    Send the XML to your client or import it into the accounting package — through your usual channel or the Peppol network.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The UBL file is named after the invoice (for example, `Factuur-F-2025-00001.xml`). Once generated, it is stored and reused on later downloads so the structured data stays consistent with the invoice.

<Tip>
Credit notes are exported as UBL too. TRONVoice automatically uses the UBL **CreditNote** document type for credit invoices, so accounting software recognises them as corrections rather than new charges.
</Tip>

## What UBL includes

The generated UBL XML contains the full structured invoice:

| Section | Contents |
|---|---|
| **Parties** | Your company and the client, each with name, address, KvK number, and VAT number. |
| **Invoice details** | Invoice number, invoice date, and due date. |
| **Line items** | Each line with description, quantity, unit price, and VAT rate. |
| **Totals** | Subtotal, VAT amounts per rate, and the invoice total. |
| **Profile** | The Peppol BIS Billing 3.0 profile identifier and VAT-based endpoint IDs. |

<Warning>
If you edit a sent invoice, TRONVoice clears the stored PDF and UBL files so they regenerate with the updated data the next time they are downloaded. Always re-download and re-deliver the UBL after editing a sent invoice.
</Warning>
