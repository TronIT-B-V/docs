TRONVoice is built for Dutch businesses, and its interface is entirely in Dutch. This documentation is in English, so you will sometimes see a Dutch label in the app that does not match the word used here. This page maps the key Dutch terms, abbreviations, and labels to clear English explanations.

<Tip>
Use your browser's find function (Cmd/Ctrl + F) to jump straight to a term.
</Tip>

## Documents & invoicing

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Factuur** | An invoice — the core document in TRONVoice, sent to a client to request payment. |
| **Conceptfactuur** | A draft invoice that is still being prepared; it has no permanent invoice number and uses a temporary `CONCEPT-XXXXX` placeholder. |
| **Creditfactuur** / **Creditnota** | A credit note — a document that reverses or corrects a previously sent invoice; the credited amount is settled against future invoices or refunded. |
| **Deelfactuur** | A partial invoice — one of several invoices created for a single accepted quote when the project is billed in phases (split invoicing). |
| **Proformafactuur** | A proforma invoice — a preview document that is not a fiscal document and carries no payment obligation; the client receives a definitive invoice after agreement. |
| **Offerte** | A quote — a priced proposal sent to a client, which can be converted into an invoice once accepted. |
| **Herinnering** | A payment reminder — an email sent automatically to a client when an invoice is overdue. |
| **Abonnement** | In invoicing, a recurring (subscription) invoice that TRONVoice generates automatically on a set interval. (See also *Abonnement* under Account & platform.) |
| **Factuurregel** | An invoice line — a single billable item on an invoice, with quantity, description, unit price, and VAT. |
| **UBL** / **e-factuur** | An electronic invoice in UBL 2.1 XML format — a machine-readable invoice that can be imported directly into accounting software. |

## Statuses

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Concept** | Draft — the document is still editable and has not been sent. |
| **Verzonden** | Sent — the document has been sent to the client or marked as sent, and has received its permanent number. |
| **Betaald** | Paid — payment has been confirmed, automatically or manually. |
| **Vervallen** | Overdue — the invoice's payment deadline has passed without payment. |
| **Geaccepteerd** | Accepted — the client has accepted the quote. |
| **Geweigerd** / **Afgewezen** | Declined — the client has rejected the quote. |

## Tax & compliance

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **BTW** | VAT (value-added tax) — the Dutch sales tax, applied at 21% (standard), 9%, or 0% depending on the goods or services. |
| **BTW-verlegd** | Reverse-charge VAT — the liability for VAT shifts to the customer, so no VAT is charged on the invoice line. |
| **BTW-vrijgesteld** | VAT-exempt — no VAT is charged because of a statutory exemption. |
| **BTW-nummer** | VAT identification number — the Dutch format is `NL123456789B01`. |
| **KvK** / **KvK-nummer** | Chamber of Commerce number — the registration number assigned by the Dutch business register (Kamer van Koophandel). |
| **VIES** | The EU's VAT Information Exchange System — TRONVoice validates a client's EU VAT number against it in real time. |
| **ICP-opgave** | The Dutch declaration of intra-community supplies — a periodic report of reverse-charge revenue to EU business customers, grouped per VAT number. |
| **G-rekening** | A blocked account — a special bank account into which part of an invoice is paid, used to safeguard VAT and payroll tax in the construction sector. |

## Clients & business data

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Klant** | A client — a customer record holding contact details, transaction history, and linked documents. |
| **Onderaannemer** | A subcontractor — a client flagged for hours registration and subcontractor invoicing; available only when the feature is enabled for your organisation. |
| **Administratie** | An administration — a separate business entity within your account, each with its own invoice series, logo, VAT settings, and client list. |
| **Organisatie** | The organisation — your overall TRONVoice account, which can contain multiple administrations. |
| **IBAN** | International Bank Account Number — the standardised bank account number used for payments. |
| **BIC** | Bank Identifier Code — the SWIFT code identifying a bank, shown alongside the IBAN. |
| **Kenteken** | A vehicle license plate — entered for registered vehicles and used to look up vehicle details from the RDW (the Dutch vehicle authority). |

## Payments & banking

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Incasso** | Direct debit — collecting an invoice amount directly from a client's bank account via SEPA. |
| **Machtiging** / **SEPA-machtiging** | A SEPA Direct Debit mandate — the one-time authorization a client gives so you can collect future payments by direct debit. |
| **Incassobatch** | A direct debit batch — a group of invoices collected together on a single chosen collection date. |
| **Vooraankondiging** | A pre-notification — the email sent to clients at least 14 days before a direct debit collection, as required by SEPA rules. |
| **Bankkoppeling** | A bank connection — a read-only link to your business bank account (via Ponto) that imports transactions for automatic matching to open invoices. |
| **MT940** | A standard bank statement file format — used to import transactions manually when you do not use a live bank connection. |

## Account & platform

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Credits** | The pay-as-you-go balance used to send documents; each sent invoice or quote costs €0.65 in credits. |
| **Opwaarderen** | To top up — adding credits to your account balance. |
| **Automatisch opwaarderen** | Auto top-up — automatically purchasing more credits when your balance drops below a set threshold. |
| **Abonnement** | A subscription — a recurring platform charge, such as for extra administrations or bank connections. (See also *Abonnement* under Documents & invoicing.) |
| **Twee-factor-authenticatie (2FA)** | Two-factor authentication — an extra login step that requires a code in addition to your password. |
| **Huisstijl** | House style / branding — the colours and logo applied to your invoices, quotes, and emails so they match your company's identity. |

<Note>
This glossary covers the most common terms. If you come across a Dutch label that is not listed here, check the relevant feature page in this documentation — the English term is usually introduced alongside its Dutch counterpart.
</Note>
