---
title: "Send invoices from your own email domain"
description: "Configure a custom email domain in TRONVoice to send invoice emails from your own address and receive client replies directly in your inbox."
---

By default, TRONVoice sends invoices from a TRONVoice email address. With a custom email domain, you can use your own business address instead — for example, `invoices@yourbusiness.com`. This makes your emails more recognizable to clients and improves deliverability.

Client replies to invoice emails are automatically received in the TRONVoice inbox, so all invoice correspondence is managed in one place.

## Why use a custom domain

- **Professional appearance** — clients see your own domain name in the sender field.
- **Better deliverability** — emails from your own verified domain are less likely to land in spam.
- **Centralized inbox** — client replies arrive directly in TRONVoice.

<Note>
  The email domain setting is per administration. If you have multiple administrations, you can set a different sender address for each. If your organization has already verified a domain in another administration, you can reuse it without re-adding the DNS records.
</Note>

## Set up your domain

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Integrations">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations** and select the relevant administration. Click the **Communication** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your domain">
    Click the **Email domain** card and enter your domain name — for example `yourbusiness.com` or a subdomain like `mail.yourbusiness.com`. Click **Add**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure DNS records">
    TRONVoice shows a set of DNS records you need to add to your domain configuration. Add these records through the control panel of your domain registrar or hosting provider.

    The records typically include an **SPF** record, a **DKIM** record, and a **DMARC** record. These are required for verification and good email deliverability.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify DNS records">
    Click **Verify** after adding the records. TRONVoice checks whether each record is correctly configured. You see the verification status for each individual record.

    You can also verify individual records separately by clicking the checkmark next to each one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Domain is active">
    Once all records are verified, your domain is active. From that point on, TRONVoice sends invoice emails from your own domain address.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  DNS changes can take up to **48 hours** to propagate globally. If verification does not succeed immediately after adding the records, wait a few hours and try again.
</Warning>

## Customize the sender address prefix

The default sender address is built as `invoices@yourdomain.com`. You can change the part before the @ sign (the prefix) after your domain is verified.

Go to the email domain settings and update the **Email prefix**. Use only lowercase letters, numbers, dots, hyphens, or underscores.

Valid prefix examples: `invoices`, `noreply`, `accounts`, `info.invoices`.

## Receive client replies in your inbox

When a client replies to an invoice email, TRONVoice receives that message automatically in the **Inbox** of your administration. Attachments in the reply are saved as well.

No extra setup is needed — inbound email is automatically active once your domain is verified.

<Tip>
  You receive a notification in TRONVoice when a new message arrives in your inbox. Check your notification settings under **Settings → Notifications** to control how you are notified.
</Tip>

## Remove your domain

Go to **Settings → Integrations → Communication → Email domain** and click **Remove**. TRONVoice disables the custom domain and sends invoice emails from the default TRONVoice address again.

<Note>
  If multiple administrations in your organization share the same domain, the DNS records and verification status are not removed from other administrations when you remove the domain from one.
</Note>
