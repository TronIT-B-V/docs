title: "Hours registration"
description: "Register subcontractor hours week by week in TRONVoice. Log hours in the weekly grid, approve them, and turn approved hours into invoices automatically."
---

**Urenregistratie** (Hours registration) lets you record the hours your subcontractors worked, week by week, in a single weekly grid. Once hours are approved, TRONVoice can turn them into invoices automatically.

You find it under **Uren** (Hours) in the navigation.

<Note>
  Hours registration is only available when **subcontractors** (onderaannemers) are enabled for your organisation. If the **Uren** item does not appear in your navigation, this feature is not active for your account — contact your administrator.
</Note>

## How it relates to subcontractors

Hours registration works with the clients in your account that are marked as **onderaannemers** (subcontractors). Each subcontractor appears as a row in the weekly grid. The hourly rate for a subcontractor is taken from that client's record, so it is pre-filled for you and can be adjusted per week.

Subcontractors can optionally be organised into **groepen** (groups). If you use groups, you can filter the grid to show one group at a time — useful when you manage many subcontractors.

## The weekly grid

The grid shows one week at a time, with a row for each active subcontractor. For each subcontractor you can record:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Aantal uren** (Hours) | The number of hours worked that week. |
| **Uurtarief** (Hourly rate) | The rate to bill. Pre-filled from the subcontractor's record. |
| **Locatie** (Location) | The location or project the hours relate to. |
| **Omschrijving** (Description) | A description of the work, used on the resulting invoice line. |
| **Notities** (Notes) | Internal notes for your own reference. |

The grid shows a running total of hours and amount for the visible rows, so you can see the week's totals at a glance.

### Navigating between weeks

Use the **previous** and **next** controls to move between weeks, or jump straight back to the current week. Each week is identified by its year and ISO week number, and the grid shows the date range it covers.

## Logging hours

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open hours registration">
    Go to **Uren** in the navigation. The grid opens on the current week.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the week">
    Navigate to the week you want to register hours for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the hours">
    For each subcontractor that worked, fill in the hours, check the hourly rate, and add a location and description where relevant.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the grid">
    Click **Save all**. TRONVoice stores every row that has hours entered. Rows left empty are not saved.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you clear the hours on a previously saved row and save again, that registration is removed — as long as it has not already been invoiced.
</Tip>

## The hours workflow

Each hours registration moves through three statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Concept** (Draft) | The hours have been entered and saved but not yet approved. Still editable. |
| **Goedgekeurd** (Approved) | The hours have been confirmed and are ready to be invoiced. |
| **Gefactureerd** (Invoiced) | An invoice has been generated for these hours. The registration is locked and can no longer be changed. |

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register and save">
    Enter and save the week's hours. They start as **Concept**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve the hours">
    When the hours are correct, use **Approve all** to move every draft registration for the week to **Goedgekeurd**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate invoices">
    Use **Generate invoices** to create an invoice for each approved registration. The registration then becomes **Gefactureerd**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What approved hours are used for

Approved hours are the basis for **subcontractor invoices**. When you generate invoices from the weekly grid, TRONVoice creates one invoice per approved registration:

- The invoice is raised **from the subcontractor to your business** (the main contractor).
- The invoice line uses the registered hours, the hourly rate, and the description — with the location appended where one was entered.
- The line uses the subcontractor's VAT setting, including **BTW verlegd** (reverse charge) where it applies.
- The invoice reference records the week and year of the hours.

Generated invoices are sent automatically. The registration is marked **Gefactureerd** and linked to its invoice.

<Note>
  A subcontractor can have invoicing days that are excluded on their client record. If you generate invoices on an excluded day for a subcontractor, those registrations are skipped — try again on an allowed day.
</Note>

<Warning>
  Once a registration is **Gefactureerd**, it is locked. Its hours, rate, and description can no longer be edited, which keeps it consistent with the invoice that was raised.
</Warning>
