A **voertuig** (vehicle) is any car, van, or motorcycle you use for trip registration. Every trip you log is linked to a vehicle, so you need to register at least one before you can record trips.

You manage vehicles under **Rittenregistratie → Voertuigen** (Trip registration → Vehicles).

## Adding a vehicle

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the vehicles list">
    Go to **Rittenregistratie → Voertuigen** and click **New vehicle**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Look up the license plate">
    Enter the **kenteken** (license plate) and use the RDW lookup. TRONVoice retrieves the vehicle's details from the Dutch vehicle authority and fills in the make, model, and vehicle type automatically. See [RDW license plate lookup](#rdw-license-plate-lookup) below.

    You can also fill in the fields manually if the lookup returns no result.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose how the vehicle is used">
    Set whether the vehicle is **zakelijk** (business) or **privé** (private). This choice changes how trips are logged for the vehicle — see [How vehicles relate to trips](#how-vehicles-relate-to-trips).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete the remaining details">
    Fill in the odometer reading and any other fields that apply. See [Vehicle fields](#vehicle-fields) for the full list.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the vehicle">
    Click **Save**. The vehicle record opens, showing its trip history and mileage statistics.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## RDW license plate lookup

The **RDW** is the *Dienst Wegverkeer* — the Dutch national vehicle authority. Every registered Dutch vehicle has a public record held by the RDW, and TRONVoice can pull that record directly into the vehicle form.

When you enter a **kenteken** and trigger the lookup, TRONVoice queries the RDW's open vehicle register and auto-fills:

| Field | Source |
|---|---|
| **Merk** (Make) | The brand, formatted in proper case (e.g. `Volkswagen`). |
| **Model** | The trade name (*handelsbenaming*), formatted in proper case. |
| **Voertuig type** (Vehicle type) | Mapped from the RDW vehicle category to a passenger car, van, motorcycle, moped, or other. |

<Note>
  A Dutch license plate is six alphanumeric characters and must contain both letters and numbers. You can enter it with or without dashes — TRONVoice normalises it automatically. If the format is invalid, the lookup will not run.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Lookup results are cached, so looking up the same plate again is instant. If the RDW has no record for the plate — for example a foreign or unregistered vehicle — enter the make, model, and type by hand.
</Tip>

## Vehicle fields

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Kenteken** (License plate) | Required. Stored in uppercase. Used to identify the vehicle and as the basis for the RDW lookup. |
| **Merk** (Make) | The vehicle brand. Filled by the RDW lookup or entered manually. |
| **Model** | The vehicle model. Filled by the RDW lookup or entered manually. |
| **Voertuig type** (Vehicle type) | One of: **Personenauto** (passenger car), **Bestelauto** (van), **Motor** (motorcycle), **Bromfiets** (moped), or **Anders** (other). |
| **Privé voertuig** (Private vehicle) | Marks the vehicle as private rather than business. Determines how trips are logged. |
| **Huidige kilometerstand** (Current odometer) | The vehicle's current mileage. Kept up to date automatically as you log business trips. |
| **Datum in gebruik** (In-use date) | The date you started using the vehicle. |
| **Actief** (Active) | Whether the vehicle is currently in use. Inactive vehicles are hidden from the trip form. |
| **Notities** (Notes) | Free-text notes for your own reference. |

## How vehicles relate to trips

Each trip is linked to exactly one vehicle, and the vehicle's **zakelijk / privé** setting controls how distance is recorded:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Business vehicle (zakelijk)" icon="briefcase">
    Trips track odometer readings. You enter the **start** and **end** odometer values, and TRONVoice calculates the distance driven. The vehicle's current odometer is updated automatically after each trip.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Private vehicle (privé)" icon="user">
    Trips record the **distance directly** — no odometer readings are needed. This supports the €0.23 per kilometre business allowance for private cars.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

When you start a new trip, TRONVoice pre-fills the last known odometer reading for the selected business vehicle, so the next trip continues seamlessly from the previous one.

On a vehicle's detail page you can see its trip history and per-year statistics — total kilometres, business kilometres, and private kilometres.

<Note>
  A vehicle marked **private** at the time of a trip stays private for that trip's record, even if you later change the vehicle's setting. This keeps historical trips accurate.
</Note>

## Deactivating and deleting vehicles

How a vehicle is removed depends on whether it has trips linked to it:

- **Vehicles with trips** cannot be deleted. Removing one instead **deactivates** it — the vehicle disappears from the trip form, but all its trips and history are preserved.
- **Vehicles without any trips** are deleted outright.

<Warning>
  Deactivating a vehicle never deletes its trips. The mileage history remains available for export and reporting.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  If you re-add a vehicle with a license plate that was previously deleted, TRONVoice restores the original record instead of creating a duplicate.
</Tip>
