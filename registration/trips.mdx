title: "Logging trips"
description: "Record your business and private trips in TRONVoice: log a journey, classify it, reuse favourite routes, copy past trips, and export your trip log for tax records."
---

A **rit** (trip) is a single journey on a given date, from a departure address to an arrival address. Logging your trips builds the **kilometeradministratie** (mileage administration) you need to substantiate business mileage to the Dutch tax authorities.

You manage trips under **Rittenregistratie** (Trip registration).

## Logging a trip

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the trip creator">
    Go to **Rittenregistratie** and click **New trip**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select a vehicle">
    Choose the **voertuig** (vehicle) for the trip. If you have only one active vehicle, it is selected automatically. The form adapts to whether the vehicle is a business or private vehicle.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the date">
    Enter the **datum** (date) of the trip. The date cannot be in the future.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter the route">
    Fill in the **vertrekadres** (departure address) and **aankomstadres** (arrival address). You can optionally add a **route omschrijving** (route description) with extra detail about the journey.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Record the distance">
    How you record distance depends on the vehicle:

    - **Business vehicle** — enter the **kilometerstand begin** (start odometer) and **kilometerstand eind** (end odometer). TRONVoice calculates the distance driven, and the end reading must be higher than the start reading. The last known odometer reading is pre-filled.
    - **Private vehicle** — enter the **gereden kilometers** (kilometres driven) directly.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Classify the trip">
    Set the **rit type** (trip type) to **zakelijk** (business) or **privé** (private). See [Business vs private trips](#business-vs-private-trips).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add purpose and client (optional)">
    Optionally link the trip to a **klant** (client) and add a **doel omschrijving** (purpose description) — for example the reason for the visit. This makes your trip log easier to verify.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the trip">
    Click **Save**. The trip is added to your registration and the vehicle's odometer is updated for business vehicles.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Business vs private trips

Every trip must be classified, because only business kilometres count towards your mileage administration:

| Trip type | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Zakelijk** (Business) | A journey made for your business. Counts as business mileage. |
| **Privé** (Private) | A personal journey. Recorded for completeness but not counted as business mileage. |

### Private detours on a business trip

A business trip can include a private detour. When a trip is classified as **zakelijk**, you can enter the **privé omweg km** (private detour kilometres) — the portion of the trip that was personal. TRONVoice subtracts the detour from the total distance, so the business kilometres for that trip reflect only the work-related part.

## Favourite routes

If you regularly drive the same journey, save it as a **favoriete route** (favourite route) so you do not have to retype the addresses each time.

A favourite route stores the departure and arrival addresses, the distance, the trip type, an optional client, and a route description. You manage them under **Rittenregistratie → Favoriete routes**.

### Saving a favourite route

There are two ways to create one:

- **From the trip form** — while logging a trip, enable **Save as favourite** and give the route a name. The route is created from the trip details when you save.
- **Directly** — go to **Rittenregistratie → Favoriete routes** and click **New route** to add one from scratch.

### Creating a trip from a favourite route

From the favourite routes list, choose **Create trip** on any route. TRONVoice opens the trip form with the addresses, distance, trip type, and client already filled in — you only need to confirm the date and odometer reading, then save.

<Tip>
  TRONVoice keeps a usage counter on each favourite route and shows your most-used routes first, so the journeys you drive most are always at the top of the list.
</Tip>

## Copying a trip

To log a trip that is similar to one you have already recorded, open the trip you want to reuse and choose **Copy**. TRONVoice opens the trip creator pre-filled with that trip's details — including the vehicle and the latest odometer reading — so you only adjust what is different and save.

This is useful for return journeys or recurring visits that are not frequent enough to warrant a saved favourite route.

## Filtering and reviewing trips

The trips list shows all your registered trips and can be filtered by:

- **Search** — departure address, arrival address, purpose, or license plate.
- **Vehicle**, **trip type**, and **client**.
- **Date range** — with quick presets for this month, last month, this quarter, and this year.

The list also shows running totals for the current filter: total kilometres, business kilometres, private kilometres, and the calculated **€0.23 per kilometre allowance** for private-vehicle business trips.

## Exporting your trip log

For your tax records or your accountant, you can export the full trip log as a spreadsheet.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the export page">
    Go to **Rittenregistratie → Export** (Exporteren).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the filters">
    Optionally choose a **start date** and **end date** to limit the export to a period, and optionally a specific **vehicle**. Leave the filters empty to export everything.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the file">
    Click **Download**. TRONVoice generates an Excel file (`.xlsx`) named after the selected period and vehicle — for example `rittenregistratie_2026-01-01_2026-03-31.xlsx`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Export per quarter or per year to match the periods your accountant works with. The trip log is a verifiable record, so exporting at regular intervals keeps your administration ready for review.
</Tip>
