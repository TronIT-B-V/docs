This page covers how to get back into your TRONVoice account: signing in, the security checks you may meet along the way, and what to do when you can't get in. If you don't have an account yet, start with [setting up your account](/account-setup).

## Sign in

Go to [tronvoice.eu](https://tronvoice.eu) and click **Inloggen** (Log in).

### With email and password

Enter your **E-mailadres** (Email address) and **Wachtwoord** (Password), then click the sign-in button. Tick **Onthoud mij** (Remember me) to stay signed in on the device you're using.

### With social login

If you signed up with a provider, use the social buttons at the top of the sign-in screen instead:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Google" icon="google">
    Sign in with your Google account.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Microsoft" icon="microsoft">
    Sign in with your Microsoft or Outlook account.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Apple" icon="apple">
    Sign in with your Apple ID.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

You're sent to the provider to authenticate, then returned to TRONVoice and signed in automatically.

<Note>
  Social-login accounts have no TRONVoice password. You always sign in through your provider — see [resetting a forgotten password](#reset-a-forgotten-password) below for what this means for you.
</Note>

## The two-factor challenge

Two-factor authentication (2FA) is mandatory on every TRONVoice account, so after entering your password you reach the **Verificatie vereist** (Verification required) screen.

Open the authenticator app on your phone and enter the current **6-digit code**, then click **Verifiëren** (Verify).

### Lost your device? Use a recovery code

If you don't have your authenticator app to hand, you can sign in with one of the recovery codes you saved when you set up 2FA.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Switch to recovery mode">
    On the challenge screen, click **Gebruik in plaats daarvan een herstelcode** (Use a recovery code instead).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a recovery code">
    Type one of your unused recovery codes (format `xxxxx-xxxxx`) and click **Verifiëren**. Each code works only once.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To switch back, click **Terug naar verificatiecode** (Back to verification code).

<Warning>
  If you've lost both your authenticator app and your recovery codes, you can't sign in on your own. Contact TRONVoice support to recover access. Once you're back in, reconfigure 2FA and save a fresh set of recovery codes from [Profile and security](/settings/profile-security).
</Warning>

## Verify your email address

New accounts created with email and password must confirm their email address before reaching the dashboard. After signing in for the first time you land on the **Verifieer je e-mail** (Verify your email) screen.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the verification email">
    TRONVoice sends a verification email to your address. Open it and click the verification link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Didn't get it?">
    Back on the verification screen, click **Verificatie-e-mail opnieuw versturen** (Resend verification email). A confirmation appears once a new link is on its way. Check your spam folder too.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once verified, you continue to the dashboard. To use a different account, click **Uitloggen en opnieuw proberen** (Log out and try again).

<Note>
  Social-login accounts (Google, Microsoft, Apple) are verified automatically by the provider, so they skip this step. The same applies to invited team members, whose email is verified the moment they accept their invitation.
</Note>

<Warning>
  If you later change your email address on your profile, the new address must be verified again before notifications are sent to it. See [Profile and security](/settings/profile-security).
</Warning>

## Reset a forgotten password

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start the reset">
    On the sign-in screen, click **Wachtwoord vergeten?** (Forgot password?). Enter your **E-mailadres** and submit the form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the reset email">
    TRONVoice emails you a password reset link. The link is valid for **60 minutes**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a new password">
    Click the link, then enter a new password and repeat it in **Bevestig nieuw wachtwoord** (Confirm new password). It must be at least 8 characters and meet TRONVoice's strength requirements.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Save the new password, then sign in with it as usual.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you signed up with Google, Microsoft, or Apple, you don't have a TRONVoice password and the **Wachtwoord vergeten** flow doesn't apply to you. Recover access through your provider instead, then sign in with the social button.
</Note>

## Confirm your password for sensitive actions

Some security-sensitive areas — such as managing two-factor authentication — ask you to re-enter your password first, even though you're already signed in. This is the **Wachtwoord bevestigen** (Confirm password) screen.

Enter your current password and continue. TRONVoice remembers the confirmation for a short while, so you won't be asked again on every action.

<Note>
  Social-login users are prompted for their TRONVoice password here as well. If you only ever use social login, contact support if you need to reach a password-protected area.
</Note>

## Accept a team invitation

If an administrator invites you to their organisation, you set up your own account from an invitation email rather than registering yourself.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the invitation email">
    You receive an email titled **Uitnodiging voor [company name]** with a personal invitation link. The link is valid for **7 days**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate your account">
    The link opens the **Account activeren** (Activate account) page, which shows the organisation you're joining. Choose a password — at least 8 characters with letters and numbers — and repeat it in **Bevestig wachtwoord** (Confirm password).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up two-factor authentication">
    Activating your account signs you in and takes you straight to the 2FA setup screen. Scan the QR code with an authenticator app and save your recovery codes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start working">
    Once 2FA is configured, you have access to the organisation with the role your administrator assigned you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If the link has expired or doesn't work, ask the administrator to resend the invitation — this issues a fresh link and resets the 7-day window.
</Warning>

For more on roles and managing invitations from the administrator's side, see [Users and roles](/settings/users-and-roles). To learn more about securing your account, see [Profile and security](/settings/profile-security).
