Each TRONVoice account can contain one or more **administrations**. An administration represents a distinct business entity — for example, a company, a subsidiary, or a department that invoices clients separately. Every administration has its own clients, invoices, quotes, products, integrations, and email templates.

You might use multiple administrations if you run more than one company, manage a holding structure, or invoice under different brands from the same account.

<Note>
  Your account includes **2 administrations at no extra cost**. Each additional administration costs €3 per month, billed monthly. Adding extra administrations also gives you a **10% discount on invoice sending costs** across your entire account.
</Note>

## Create an administration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the administrations page">
    Go to **Settings → Administrations** and click **New administration**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search via KvK (optional)">
    Use the KvK search field to find your company by name or registration number. Selecting a result auto-fills the company name, registration number, and address. You can skip this step and fill in the fields manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter company details">
    Fill in the required and optional fields:

    | Field | Required | Notes |
    |---|---|---|
    | Company name | Yes | Appears on all invoices and emails |
    | KvK number | No | 8-digit Chamber of Commerce number |
    | VAT number | No | Dutch format: `NL123456789B01` — validated via VIES if entered |
    | Address | No | Street and house number |
    | Postcode | No | Dutch format: `1234 AB` |
    | City | No | |
    | Country | No | Defaults to the Netherlands |
    | IBAN | No | Used on invoice PDFs and payment footers |
    | BIC | No | |

  </Step>
  <Step title="Set invoice numbering">
    Configure a **prefix** and **starting number** for your invoices.

    You can include dynamic placeholders in the prefix:
    - `[jaar]` — replaced with the current year (e.g. `2026`)
    - `[maand]` — replaced with the current month (e.g. `05`)

    **Example:** A prefix of `F[jaar]-` with starting number `0001` produces invoice numbers like `F2026-00001`.

    A live preview of the next invoice number updates as you type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure invoice defaults">
    Set the defaults that will apply to every new invoice in this administration:

    - **Payment term** — number of days until the invoice is due (default: 30 days)
    - **Default VAT rate** — 21%, 9%, or 0%
    - **VAT shifted** — enable if you normally apply reverse charge VAT
    - **VAT exempt** — enable for exempt sectors such as healthcare or education
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Create administration**. The administration is immediately available in your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Switch between administrations

If you have multiple administrations, a switcher appears in the navigation bar. Click your current administration name to open the switcher and select a different one.

Each administration is fully isolated — switching changes which clients, invoices, quotes, and settings you are working with.

## Default settings

After creating an administration, you can adjust the following defaults at any time under **Settings → Administration → Edit**:

- **Invoice prefix** — The prefix applied to all invoice numbers (supports `[jaar]` and `[maand]` placeholders)
- **Payment term** — Default number of days until payment is due
- **Invoice footer** — Text printed at the bottom of every invoice PDF. Use `{betalingstermijn}` to insert the payment term automatically
- **Admin email addresses** — One or more email addresses (comma-separated or one per line) that receive copies of invoice notifications, for example your accountant

<Tip>
  If you use the same footer text across all your administrations, set a default footer at the organisation level. New administrations will inherit it automatically.
</Tip>

## Archive and restore an administration

Archiving removes an administration from the active switcher and prevents new invoices from being created in it, but preserves all historical data.

**To archive:**
1. Go to the administration you want to archive.
2. Open **Settings → Administration → Edit**.
3. Uncheck **Administration is active** and save.

**To restore:**
1. Go to **Settings → Administrations** — archived administrations are listed separately.
2. Open the archived administration and check **Administration is active** again.

<Warning>
  Archiving an administration does not delete any data. All invoices, clients, and documents remain accessible.
</Warning>
