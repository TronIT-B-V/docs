Each administration in TRONVoice has its own branding settings. These control how your invoice PDFs and outgoing emails look — your logo, the PDF layout, and the colours used throughout.

Go to **Settings → Huisstijl** for the administration you want to configure.

## Upload your company logo

Your logo appears on invoice PDFs and in the header of outgoing emails.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open huisstijl settings">
    Go to **Settings → Huisstijl** for the relevant administration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload a logo">
    Click the logo upload area and select an image from your computer. Alternatively, if you fill in your **website** field in the administration settings, TRONVoice will automatically fetch your logo from the domain.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The logo is applied immediately to new invoice PDFs and emails.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Supported formats:** JPG, PNG, and SVG are recommended. Use a version of your logo with a transparent background (PNG or SVG) for the best result on white invoice PDFs.

**Where the logo appears:**
- Top of all invoice and credit note PDFs
- Top of all quote PDFs
- Header of all outgoing client emails

<Tip>
  Use a horizontal or square logo for best results. Very tall logos may be scaled down to fit the invoice header area.
</Tip>

## Choose an invoice PDF template

TRONVoice offers ten invoice PDF templates. Each template has a distinct layout; all of them apply your logo and custom colours.

| Template | Description |
|---|---|
| **Standaard** | Classic layout with logo on the left and details on the right |
| **Modern** | Clean layout with a coloured header |
| **Minimaal** | Ultra-clean design with generous white space |
| **Klassiek** | Traditional and formal design |
| **Zakelijk** | Corporate look with a coloured header band |
| **Professioneel** | Stylish design with an accent line |
| **Horizontaal** | Metadata on one row with an accent stripe |
| **Accent** | Bold summary section with highlight colours |
| **Compact** | Space-saving two-column layout |
| **Clean** | Modern card-style line item display |

**To select a template:**

1. Scroll through the template carousel in the **Factuur sjabloon** section.
2. Click a template thumbnail to select it. A checkmark appears on the selected template.
3. Click **Vergroot** on hover to open a full-size PDF preview before committing.
4. The live preview panel on the right updates automatically to show how your invoices will look with the selected template and current colours.

### Separate quote template

By default, quotes use the same template as invoices. If you want a different look for quotes, enable **Gebruik een apart sjabloon voor offertes** and select a separate template from the quote template carousel.

## Customise invoice colours

You can set four independent colours for invoice PDFs:

| Colour | Used for |
|---|---|
| **Primary colour** | Header background, total row |
| **Secondary colour** | Credit invoice accents |
| **Accent colour** | Discount rows and highlights |
| **Text colour** | Body text throughout the PDF |

Click the colour swatch next to each setting to open a colour picker, or type a hex value directly (e.g. `#2563eb`). Changes are reflected immediately in the live preview.

To revert all colours to the TRONVoice defaults, click **Standaard kleuren**.

<Note>
  Invoice colours are separate from email colours. Email colours are configured in the same **Huisstijl** page under the email templates section.
</Note>

## TRONVoice footer in emails

By default, a small "Sent with TRONVoice" footer is included at the bottom of all outgoing client emails. You can remove it by unchecking **TRONVoice footer tonen in uitgaande e-mails** on the Huisstijl page.

## Live preview

The Huisstijl page includes a sticky live preview panel on the right side of the screen. The preview renders a real invoice PDF using your current template selection, colours, and logo, so you can see exactly what your clients will receive before saving.

Use the language selector in the preview panel to check how the invoice looks in different languages.
