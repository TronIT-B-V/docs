title: "Profile and security"
description: "Manage your personal TRONVoice account: edit your profile, upload an avatar, change your password, manage mandatory two-factor authentication, and set notification preferences."
---

Your profile page holds the settings that belong to you personally — not to your organisation or administration. To open it, click your name in the top-right corner and choose **Mijn profiel** (My profile).

The page has five sections: profile picture, profile information, password, notifications, and two-factor authentication.

## Profile picture

A profile picture (**Profielfoto**) personalises your account and appears in the top bar and on shared screens.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload a photo">
    Click **Foto uploaden** (Upload photo) and select an image. JPG, PNG, GIF, and WebP are supported, up to a maximum of 2 MB.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove a photo">
    Once a photo is set, a **Verwijderen** (Remove) link appears next to the upload button. Click it to go back to your initials.
  </Step>
</Steps>

If you don't upload a photo, TRONVoice shows your initials on a coloured background instead.

<Note>
  If you signed up with Google, Microsoft, or Apple, your profile picture from that provider is used automatically. You can still upload your own to replace it.
</Note>

## Profile information

The **Profielgegevens** (Profile details) section holds your name and email address.

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Naam** (Name) | Your full name, shown across the app. Required. |
| **E-mailadres** (Email address) | Used to log in and to receive notifications. Required and must be unique. |

Edit the fields and click **Opslaan** (Save).

<Warning>
  If you change your email address, it is marked as unverified until you confirm it. An **E-mailadres niet geverifieerd** (Email not verified) notice appears below the field until you do.
</Warning>

This section also shows your **Organisatie** (Organisation) and your **Rol** (Role) — Eigenaar (Owner), Beheerder (Admin), or Lid (Member). These are read-only; they are managed by your organisation administrator.

## Change your password

In the **Wachtwoord wijzigen** (Change password) section:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm your current password">
    Enter your existing password in **Huidig wachtwoord** (Current password).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a new password">
    Type your new password, then repeat it in **Bevestig nieuw wachtwoord** (Confirm new password). The password must meet TRONVoice's strength requirements.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Wachtwoord wijzigen** (Change password).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you log in with Google, Microsoft, or Apple, you authenticate through that provider and do not need a TRONVoice password.
</Note>

## Two-factor authentication

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a second step to logging in: a 6-digit code from an authenticator app on your phone.

<Warning>
  2FA is **mandatory** on TRONVoice. It is set up during registration before you can reach the dashboard. The settings below let you maintain it — they do not let you remove the protection.
</Warning>

When 2FA is active, the **Tweefactorauthenticatie** section confirms **Tweefactorauthenticatie is ingeschakeld** (Two-factor authentication is enabled) and offers three actions. Each one asks you to confirm your password first.

### View recovery codes

Recovery codes are one-time codes that let you log in if you lose access to your authenticator app.

Click **Bekijk herstelcodes** (View recovery codes) and confirm your password to see your current codes.

<Tip>
  Store your recovery codes somewhere safe — a password manager or a printed copy kept offline. Each code works only once.
</Tip>

### Regenerate recovery codes

If you've used some of your codes, or think they may be exposed, generate a fresh set.

Click **Nieuwe herstelcodes genereren** (Generate new recovery codes) and confirm your password. TRONVoice immediately shows the new codes.

<Warning>
  Regenerating invalidates your old recovery codes straight away. Save the new set before closing the window.
</Warning>

### Reconfigure with a new device

If you get a new phone, you need to reconnect 2FA to the new authenticator app.

Use the **Tweefactorauthenticatie** action to reconfigure. After confirming your password, TRONVoice removes your old 2FA setup and takes you to the setup screen, where you scan a fresh QR code with your new device and save a new set of recovery codes.

<Note>
  Because 2FA is mandatory, you must complete the new setup before you can return to the dashboard. Keep your new device to hand before you start.
</Note>

## Notification preferences

The **Notificaties** (Notifications) section controls which emails TRONVoice sends you.

| Notification | When you receive it |
|---|---|
| **Inbox e-mailnotificaties** (Inbox email notifications) | An email is sent whenever a new message arrives in your TRONVoice inbox. |

Use the toggle to turn the notification on or off, then click **Opslaan** (Save).

<Tip>
  Turning a notification off only stops the email. New inbox messages still appear in the app, with an unread count next to **Inbox** in the sidebar.
</Tip>
