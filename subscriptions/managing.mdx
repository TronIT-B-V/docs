title: "Manage recurring invoices"
description: "Activate or pause subscriptions, generate invoices on demand, generate all due invoices, edit a subscription, and use bulk actions."
---

After a subscription is set up, you manage it from its detail page or the Subscriptions list. This page covers everything you do once a recurring invoice is running.

## Activating and pausing

A subscription only generates invoices while it is **active** (**actief**). Pausing one stops all generation — its next invoice date does not advance while it is inactive.

To change the status, open the subscription and use the **Active** toggle, or select it in the Subscriptions list and use the **Activate** or **Deactivate** bulk action.

<Tip>
When you resume a paused subscription, check its **next invoice date**. If that date is already in the past, the subscription is immediately due and will generate an invoice on the next run.
</Tip>

## Generating an invoice manually

To generate an invoice from a single subscription right away — regardless of its next invoice date:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the subscription">
    Go to **Subscriptions** and open the one you want to invoice. It must be **active** and have at least one priced line (note lines alone are not enough).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the invoice">
    Click **Generate invoice now** (**Genereer factuur**). TRONVoice creates the invoice, copies the lines with date variables replaced, and advances the next invoice date by one interval.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sent or draft">
    If **Auto-send** is on and you have credits, the invoice is emailed to the recipients you configured. Otherwise it is saved as a **draft** for you to send manually.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
If auto-send was enabled but the invoice was still saved as a draft, the reason is shown — usually insufficient credits or a day excluded from invoicing for the client.
</Note>

## Generating all due invoices

To process every subscription that has reached its schedule at once, use **Generate due invoices** (**Genereer verschuldigde facturen**) from the Subscriptions list.

This finds all **active** subscriptions whose next invoice date is on or before today (and whose end date hasn't passed), and generates an invoice for each. Subscriptions without priced lines are skipped and reported.

After running, a summary tells you how many invoices were generated, how many were emailed, and how many were saved as drafts because of insufficient credits.

<Tip>
Run **Generate due invoices** at the start of each billing period to catch up everything that's due in one action.
</Tip>

## Bulk actions

In the Subscriptions list, select one or more subscriptions to apply a bulk action:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Activate" icon="toggle-right">
    Resume multiple paused subscriptions at once.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Deactivate" icon="toggle-left">
    Pause multiple active subscriptions at once.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Generate invoices" icon="file-text">
    Generate an invoice for each selected **active** subscription, regardless of its due date. Subscriptions without priced lines are skipped.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Delete" icon="trash-2">
    Remove the selected subscriptions.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
Deleting a subscription stops it from generating any further invoices. Invoices already generated from it are kept and remain linked to the subscription.
</Warning>

## Editing a subscription

Open a subscription and click **Edit** to change any of its settings — name, reference, client, billing interval, dates, invoice lines, discount, and the active and sending options.

Changes apply to **future** invoices only. Invoices already generated are not affected, because each invoice keeps its own copy of the lines and totals from the moment it was created.

<Note>
Editing the **next invoice date** changes when the subscription is next due. Setting it to a past date makes the subscription due immediately.
</Note>

## Viewing generated invoices

Every invoice generated from a subscription stays linked to it. Open a subscription and find the **Recent invoices** section to see the most recent invoices, each with a direct link.

Generated invoices carry a note identifying the subscription they came from, so you can always trace an invoice back to its source.
