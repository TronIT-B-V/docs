---
title: "Recurring invoices with subscriptions"
description: "Automate invoice generation for clients you bill on a fixed schedule. Set an interval, add invoice lines, and let TRONVoice generate invoices on the due date."
---

A subscription is a recurring invoice template linked to a client. You define the invoice lines and a billing interval once, and TRONVoice generates a new invoice automatically each time the due date is reached — weekly, monthly, quarterly, or on any other schedule you set.

## Creating a subscription

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open subscriptions">
    Go to **Subscriptions** in the main navigation and click **New subscription**.

    You can also create a subscription directly from a client record — this pre-selects the client and administration automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the basic details">
    | Field | Notes |
    |---|---|
    | Name | Internal name for this subscription, e.g. "Monthly hosting fee". |
    | Reference | Optional reference number shown on generated invoices. |
    | Client | The client this subscription belongs to. |
    | Start date | The date the subscription becomes active. |
    | First invoice date | The date the first invoice will be generated. |
    | End date | Optional. Leave empty for an open-ended subscription. |
    | Interval | How often invoices are generated (see intervals below). |
    | Active | Inactive subscriptions never generate invoices. |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a billing interval">
    | Interval | Description |
    |---|---|
    | Daily | One invoice per day |
    | Weekly | One invoice per week |
    | Monthly | One invoice per month |
    | Quarterly | One invoice every three months |
    | Yearly | One invoice per year |
    | Every 2 years | One invoice every two years |

    For custom intervals (e.g. every 2 weeks), set the interval to *Weekly* and enter `2` as the interval value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add invoice lines">
    Add one or more lines to the subscription. Each line can reference a product from your product catalogue or be entered as a custom description with a price.

    Line fields:
    - Description
    - Quantity and unit
    - Unit price
    - VAT rate (inherited from the client's VAT settings by default)
    - Discount percentage (per line)

    You can also add **note lines** — text-only lines that appear on the invoice but have no price.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure sending options">
    | Option | Description |
    |---|---|
    | Auto-send | Automatically send the generated invoice by email on the due date. |
    | Send to administration | Send a copy to your own administration email. |
    | Send to client | Send the invoice to the client's email address. |

    If auto-send is off, invoices are created as drafts and you send them manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the subscription">
    Click **Save**. The subscription is now active and will generate its first invoice on the date you set.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Automatic invoice generation

When a subscription's next invoice date is today or in the past, TRONVoice marks it as **due**. Invoices are generated in one of two ways:

1. **Automatic** — if auto-send is enabled and your account has sufficient credits, TRONVoice generates the invoice and sends it to the client without any action from you.
2. **Manual** — if auto-send is off, you generate the invoice yourself (see below).

After an invoice is generated, the subscription's next invoice date advances automatically to the following period.

<Note>
  If a client has specific days excluded from invoicing, auto-send is skipped on those days. The invoice is created as a draft instead, and you can send it manually.
</Note>

## Generating an invoice manually

To generate an invoice from a single subscription immediately — regardless of the due date:

1. Open the subscription.
2. Click **Generate invoice now**.

The invoice is created and, if auto-send is enabled and you have credits, sent to the client right away. If not, the invoice is saved as a draft.

## Pausing and resuming a subscription

You can pause a subscription at any time by toggling it to **inactive**. While inactive, no invoices are generated, and the next invoice date does not advance.

To pause a subscription:
- Open the subscription and click the **Active** toggle, or
- From the subscriptions list, select the subscription and use the **Deactivate** bulk action.

To resume, set the subscription back to active. The next invoice will generate on the next due date.

<Tip>
  If you resume a subscription after a long pause, check the next invoice date. If it has passed, the subscription will be due immediately and an invoice will be generated on the next run.
</Tip>

## Bulk actions

From the subscriptions list, select one or more subscriptions to apply bulk actions:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Generate due invoices" icon="file-text">
    Generate invoices for all subscriptions that are currently due. Useful at the start of a billing period.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Activate" icon="toggle-right">
    Resume multiple paused subscriptions at once.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Deactivate" icon="toggle-left">
    Pause multiple active subscriptions at once.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The **Generate due invoices** action processes all subscriptions with a next invoice date on or before today. For each one, it creates an invoice and sends it if auto-send is enabled.

## Viewing generated invoices

Every invoice generated from a subscription is linked back to it. Open a subscription and scroll to the **Recent invoices** section to see the last invoices that were generated, with direct links to each one.
