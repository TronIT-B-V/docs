title: "Convert a quote to an invoice"
description: "Turn an accepted quote into an invoice, and manage the other status actions: mark sent, accepted or declined, archive, duplicate, and scheduled sending."
---

Once a client accepts a quote, you can convert it into an invoice without re-entering any data. This page also covers the other status actions available on a quote.

## Converting an accepted quote

A quote can only be invoiced when its status is **Accepted** (**geaccepteerd**).

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the accepted quote">
    Go to **Quotes** and open a quote with status **Accepted**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Convert to invoice">
    Click **Convert to invoice** (**Factureren**). TRONVoice copies every line item — descriptions, quantities, unit prices, per-line VAT rates, discounts, location groups, and note lines — into a new invoice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and send">
    The new invoice opens as a **draft** with a concept number, today's date as the invoice date, and a due date based on your administration's payment term. The invoice **Reference** field is set to `Offerte [number]`. Check the details, then send the invoice to the client.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Each invoice created from a quote stays linked to it. The conversion is logged in the quote's **Activity** tab.
</Note>

### Split invoicing

If you set up a **split invoicing schedule** on the quote, the accepted quote shows each part separately. When converting you can:

- **Invoice a single part** — creates an invoice for one part by its percentage or fixed amount.
- **Invoice the remaining parts** — combines all parts that haven't been invoiced yet into one invoice labelled `Restant facturatie`.
- **Invoice the full amount** — ignores the schedule and invoices the entire quote in one go. Choosing this clears the schedule.

Each invoiced part is marked as done so it can't be billed twice. Quotes with mixed VAT rates get a proportional invoice line per rate, keeping the tax calculation correct.

<Warning>
Stock is checked at conversion time. If a line uses a product that blocks sales on insufficient stock and there isn't enough, the invoice is not created and an error explains which product is short.
</Warning>

## Status actions

Beyond converting, a quote supports several status actions from its detail page.

### Send the quote

Sending a **draft** (**concept**) quote emails it to the client with the PDF attached and a personal acceptance link, and moves the status to **Sent** (**verzonden**). The client must have an email address, and your account needs sufficient credits.

### Mark as sent

Use **Mark as sent** (**Markeer als verzonden**) when you delivered the quote yourself — for example, printed or handed over in person. This moves a **draft** quote to **Sent** without emailing anything, generates the acceptance link, and produces a PDF without the `CONCEPT` watermark so you can download a clean copy.

### Mark as accepted

Use **Mark as accepted** when a client confirmed outside the app, such as by phone. This is only available on a **Sent** quote. You can optionally record the **name** and **email** of the person who accepted. The status moves to **Accepted** and the quote becomes ready to invoice.

### Mark as declined

Use **Mark as declined** to record a rejection on a **Sent** quote. A **reason** is required. The status moves to **Declined** (**geweigerd**) and the reason is stored on the quote.

<Tip>
After a decline, the quote can't be reopened for acceptance. Use **Duplicate** to start a fresh proposal from the same content.
</Tip>

### Archive and unarchive

**Archive** (**Archiveren**) hides a quote from your default Quotes view without deleting it. Open an archived quote and click **Unarchive** (**Herstellen**) to bring it back.

### Duplicate

**Duplicate** (**Dupliceren**) creates a full copy of the quote — including all line items — as a new **draft**. Use it to revise a declined proposal or to base a new quote on a past one.

## Scheduling a quote send

Instead of sending immediately, you can schedule a **draft** quote to go out automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the draft quote">
    Scheduling is only available while the quote is still a **draft**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a date and time">
    Choose a **scheduled date** (today or later) and a **time** in 15-minute steps. The combined moment must be in the future. The client needs an email address and your account needs sufficient credits.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    TRONVoice emails the quote automatically at the scheduled moment. Until then the quote stays a draft and you can still edit it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To stop a scheduled send, open the quote and click **Cancel scheduled send** (**Annuleer geplande verzending**). Sending the quote manually also clears any schedule.

## Bulk actions

From the Quotes list, select multiple quotes to act on them at once:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Send" icon="send">
    Sends all selected **draft** quotes. Quotes without a client email address are skipped.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Download" icon="download">
    Downloads the selected quotes as PDFs — a single file for one quote, or a ZIP archive for several.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Delete" icon="trash-2">
    Deletes selected quotes. Only **draft** quotes can be deleted.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
