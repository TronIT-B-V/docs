---
title: "How clients accept or decline a quote online"
description: "How clients accept or decline quotes in TRONVoice, what happens after they respond, and how to convert an accepted quote to an invoice."
---

When you send a quote, TRONVoice generates a unique, secure link for your client. No login is required—the client simply clicks the link to view and respond to the proposal.

## How the client receives the quote

The quote email includes:

- A PDF attachment of the quote
- A personal online acceptance link (the `{acceptatie_url}` variable in your email template)

When the client clicks the link, they land on a branded acceptance page that shows your company name or logo.

<Note>
The acceptance link is unique to each quote and cannot be guessed or reused. TRONVoice logs every time the client opens the page or views the PDF, which you can see in the quote's **Activity** tab.
</Note>

## The client's acceptance flow

On the acceptance page, the client sees:

1. An embedded PDF preview of the quote (with a link to open it in a new tab)
2. A short summary showing the quote number, validity date, and total amount
3. An acceptance form

To accept, the client fills in:

- **Their name**
- **Their email address**
- **A digital signature** — drawn with a mouse or finger on a signature pad

After submitting, the client sees a confirmation screen. The signed quote is stored and you receive a notification by email.

To decline, the client clicks **Decline** and can optionally leave a reason.

## What happens after a response

### When the client accepts

- The quote status changes to **Accepted**
- You receive a notification email
- The quote detail page shows the acceptance details: name, email, date, IP address, and the digital signature
- The quote is ready to be converted to an invoice

### When the client declines

- The quote status changes to **Declined**
- You receive a notification email
- The decline reason (if provided) is visible on the quote detail page
- You can create a new quote for the same client to revise your proposal

<Tip>
Check the **Activity** tab on a sent quote to see exactly when the client opened the page and viewed the PDF, even before they respond.
</Tip>

## Converting an accepted quote to an invoice

Once a quote is accepted, a **Convert to invoice** action becomes available on the quote detail page.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the accepted quote">
    Go to **Quotes** and open the quote with status **Accepted**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Convert to invoice">
    Click **Convert to invoice** (or **Factureren** in the action menu). TRONVoice creates a new invoice with all line items, totals, VAT rates, and client details copied over from the quote.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and send the invoice">
    The new invoice opens as a draft. Check the details, adjust the invoice date or due date if needed, then send it to the client.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Split invoicing

If you set up a split invoicing schedule on the quote, each part is listed separately on the accepted quote page. You can:

- Create an invoice for a **single part** by clicking **Invoice** on that part
- Create one invoice for **all remaining parts** at once
- Ignore the schedule and **invoice the full amount** in one go (only available before any part has been invoiced)

A progress bar shows how much of the total has been invoiced so far.

## Handling expired quotes

A quote expires automatically when the validity date passes. The client's acceptance link becomes inactive and shows an expiry message.

To give a client more time, edit the quote and extend the **Valid until** date. The updated quote can then be re-sent or the client can be directed back to the same acceptance link.

## Handling declined quotes

After a decline, the original quote cannot be reopened for acceptance. To make a new proposal:

1. Open the declined quote
2. Click **Duplicate** to create a copy as a new draft
3. Adjust the content based on the client's feedback
4. Send the new quote

The activity log on both quotes keeps a full history of what was sent and when.
