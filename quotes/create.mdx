---
title: "Create and send a quote in TRONVoice"
description: "Step-by-step guide to creating a quote in TRONVoice: choosing a client, adding line items, setting a validity date, previewing, and sending."
---

You can create a new quote from the **Quotes** section in the sidebar. Each new quote starts as a draft, so you can take your time building it before anything is sent to a client.

## Create a quote

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a client">
    Open **Quotes** and click **New quote**. Search for an existing client or create a new one. The client's name, address, and preferred language are pulled in automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose a template or start from scratch">
    If you have quote templates set up, you can select one to pre-fill line items and attach a cover page or appendix PDF. If no templates are available, the quote starts with a blank line item list.

    <Tip>
      Templates save time when you regularly quote similar work. You can manage them under **Settings → Quote templates**.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add line items">
    Add one or more lines to your quote. For each line you can set:

    - **Description** — what you're offering
    - **Quantity and unit** — for example, `10 hours` or `1 piece`
    - **Unit price**
    - **VAT rate** — per line (standard, reversed, or exempt)
    - **Discount** — a percentage off that line

    You can also add **note lines** to include clarifying text between items without affecting the totals.

    To group lines by location or project phase, fill in the **Location group** field on a line. A header row will appear in the PDF for each unique group.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set the validity date">
    The **Valid until** date controls when the client's acceptance link expires. TRONVoice fills this in automatically based on your administration's default validity period, but you can change it for any individual quote.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a reference (optional)">
    Enter a **Reference** if your client has a purchase order number or internal reference they need on the document.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Attach files (optional)">
    Upload PDF attachments to include with the quote. You can choose to merge these into a single PDF or keep them as separate files.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure split invoicing (optional)">
    If the project will be billed in phases, set up a **split invoicing schedule** by defining the parts as percentages. After the client accepts, you can create a separate invoice for each part.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Preview and send">
    Click **Preview PDF** to see exactly what the client will receive. When you're ready:

    - Click **Send** to email the quote immediately, or
    - Set a **Scheduled send date** to have TRONVoice send it automatically at a future date and time.

    The email is sent in the client's preferred language, with the PDF attached and a personal online acceptance link included.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Draft autosave

TRONVoice saves your draft automatically as you work. If you close the page before sending, you can return to the quote at any time from the Quotes list and pick up where you left off.

## Discounts

You can apply a discount at two levels:

- **Line level** — a percentage off a single line item
- **Quote level** — an overall percentage discount applied to the subtotal after all lines are summed

Both discounts appear on the PDF and are reflected in the totals.

## Templates

Quote templates let you define a set of standard line items, a cover page PDF, and an appendix PDF that can be loaded into any new quote. Templates are shared across your administration.

To manage templates, go to **Settings → Quote templates**. Each template can be marked active or inactive, and you can duplicate a template to create a variant.

<Note>
Applying a template to a new quote copies the template's lines and files into the quote. Subsequent changes to the template do not affect quotes that were already created from it.
</Note>
